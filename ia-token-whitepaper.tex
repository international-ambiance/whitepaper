\begin{equation}
\text{Voting Power} = \sqrt{\text{Token Balance} \times \text{Participation Multiplier}}

\subsection{Howey Test Analysis}

For US readers wondering about securities law, here's how IA differs from a security under the Howey Test:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Investment of money}: Yes, you're buying tokens
    \item \textbf{Common enterprise}: Debatable—community is collective, but no profit-sharing
    \item \textbf{Expectation of profit}: \textbf{NO}—explicitly disclaimed throughout this document
    \item \textbf{Derived from efforts of others}: \textbf{NO}—value comes from community participation and events, not team efforts to increase value
\end{enumerate}

We believe IA is not a security. But we're not your lawyers, and laws vary by jurisdiction.

\subsection{Know Your Customer (KYC) and Anti-Money Laundering (AML)}

For regulatory compliance, we may implement:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]

We'll minimize intrusive requirements while staying compliant.
